#Function written to determine outliers, upper and lower bounds, and iqr for various variables in housing dataset.
def detect_outlier(df):
    # find q1 and q3 values
    q1, q3 = np.percentile(sorted(df), [25, 75])
 
    # compute IRQ
    iqr = q3 - q1
 
    # find lower and upper bounds
    lower_bound = q1 - 1.5 * int(iqr)
    upper_bound = q3 + 1.5 * int(iqr)
 
    outliers = [x for x in df if x <= lower_bound or x >= upper_bound]
 
    return outliers

# Using a for statement, replaing all outliers found in price column of dataset with null values.
for x in ['price']:
    q75,q25 = np.percentile(df.loc[:,x],[75,25])
    iqr= q75-q25

    max = q75+(1.5*iqr)
    min = q25-(1.5*iqr)
    df.loc[df[x] < min,x] = np.nan
    df.loc[df[x] > max,x] = np.nan

Introduction: A dataset HousingNew which has 546 rows and 9 columns: ID, Lotsize, Bedrooms,Bathrms,Stories, GaragePl,Recroom,Fullbase, and Airco. The following concepts were applied in the exploratory data analysis: key statistics, Boxplot plotting, scatter plotting, correlation analysis, and IQR and outlier determinations.

Analysis: The following Python libraries were imported to do the analysis: Pandas, Numpy, SciPy. Stats, and Matplotlib. The data was imported using pandas. There were more new houses which did not include a full basement, air conditioning, a garage place or a rec room. 65% of the HousingNew dataset does not have a full basement. 68% of the HousingNew dataset do not have air conditioning. 82% do not have a rec room, and 55% do not have a garage place. These insights were determined based on the numbers from the value counts command on Python. Mode is the most observed value in a data. The highest number of houses in the dataset had 3 bedrooms and 1 bathroom. The lowest number of houses in the dataset came with 4 bathrooms and 1 bedroom. These insights give clarity to the type of houses in the area and can help those interested in the data, for example real estate companies or investors.
The mean price is defined as the mathematical average of the data. The mean price was 68,121, minimum price was 25000, and the maximum price was 190000. This indicates that there is a high variability of prices within the dataset. When building a machine learning model , statistics is important to consider. The mean, maximum values, and minimum values insight will tell how much the variability is affecting the mean price, and what kinds of differences are there between the houses in various price ranges (ie.do the houses come with/without a garage? How many bedrooms? How many bathrooms? Does it include a rec room? etc.) Standard Deviation describes the spread of the observation from the mean. A high standard deviation indicates that the values are spread over a wide range. A low standard deviation indicates that the values are closer to the mean(average) value. The standard deviation for price variable is 26702.7 which is far from the mean value indicating a large variation in prices. The same statistics were carried out for the variable lotsize, and it was found that the mean lotsize was 5150, the minimum lotsize was 1650, and the maximum lotsize was 16200. The standard deviation for lotsize 2168.15 which is not far from the mean. This indicates that the various lot sizes have less variation and are closer in number to one another. The coefficient of variation is a measure ratio of the standard deviation in relation to the mean. The larger the coefficient of variation value, the higher the data has variability. The coefficient of variation for price and lot size were 39% and 42% respectively. This insight is helpful to consider when developing a machine learning model because there is a close degree of variability between the two variables. Linearity models can be done with these variables.Correlation ranges from -1 to 1.A negative correlation indicates that as one variable goes up, the other goes down.A correlation of 0 means that two variables are not related at all. A correlation of 1 is perfect correlation, and means that as the ﬁrst variable changes, the second changes in the same direction, though not necessarily by the same amount After running the correlation command on Python, price and lot size have the highest correlation of 0.53.
In data analysis, an outlier Is defined as an untypical observed data point in a given distribution of data points. An outlier can be visualized using a boxplot and determining the upper bound,lower bound, and interquartile range. Any values that fall over the maximum value of the boxplot, and below the minimum value of the boxplot are considered outliers. A function was written in python to determine the outlier for various variables and their boxplots were plotted. Since the highest correlation was determined between the price and lotsize variables, all the outliers were replaced with null values and the key statistics were recalculated. The new mean price was 65754, and the new price standard deviation was 22791. The new mean lot size was 4980.8, and the new lot size standard deviation was 1853.5. Outlier detection is an important insight before developing a machine learning model because outliers can mislead the training process. Outliers will cause less accuracy, longer training times, and incorrect prediction results.
